<p><ul><li>Molecular Property Spectrometer Sensors</li><li>Non-Dispersive Infrared (NDIR) Sensors</li><li>Catalytic Gas Sensors</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) sensor market includes several types, each serving distinct applications. Molecular Property Spectrometer Sensors detect LPG through molecular interactions, providing high sensitivity. Non-Dispersive Infrared (NDIR) Sensors measure gas concentration by analyzing infrared light absorption specific to LPG. Catalytic Gas Sensors operate by measuring changes in resistance caused by gas oxidation, making them reliable for detecting flammable gases. Other types may incorporate technologies like electrochemical sensors or photoionization detectors, expanding the market's versatility and application scope.</p></p>
