<p><p>In the conductive ink printer market, two primary types are prevalent: Continuous Inkjet (CIJ) and Drop-on-Demand (DOD) inkjet technologies. CIJ printers maintain a continuous flow of ink droplets, enabling high-speed printing and precise patterns on various substrates, ideal for large-scale production. In contrast, DOD printers release ink droplets only when needed, allowing for greater control over droplet size and placement, which is beneficial for intricate designs and smaller production runs. Both types cater to distinct industrial applications in electronics and sensors.</p></p>
