<p><p>Surgical drains are devices used post-surgery to remove unwanted fluids from a wound, preventing complications such as infection or hematoma. The market primarily consists of two types: passive drains and active drains. Passive drains rely on gravity and natural pressure to facilitate drainage, commonly seen in simple procedures. In contrast, active drains, often equipped with suction mechanisms, create negative pressure, ensuring effective fluid removal in complex surgeries. Both types cater to varying clinical requirements, shaping a diverse market.</p></p>
