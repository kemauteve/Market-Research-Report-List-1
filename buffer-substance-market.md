<p><ul><li>Acidic</li><li>Alkaline</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Buffer substances are crucial in maintaining pH stability in various environments, and they can be categorized into acidic and alkaline markets. Acidic buffers resist changes in pH by neutralizing added bases, typically composed of a weak acid and its conjugate base. In contrast, alkaline buffers maintain pH in basic environments, consisting of a weak base and its conjugate acid. Both markets serve essential roles across industries like pharmaceuticals, biochemistry, and food processing, where precise pH control is vital for product efficacy and stability.</p></p>
