<p><ul><li>Ischemic</li><li>Hemorrhagic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The stroke insurance market is primarily divided into two types: ischemic and hemorrhagic. Ischemic stroke insurance focuses on coverage for strokes caused by blockages in blood vessels, leading to reduced blood flow to the brain. In contrast, hemorrhagic stroke insurance addresses strokes resulting from the rupture of blood vessels, causing bleeding in or around the brain. Each market type offers tailored policies that cater to the specific risks, treatments, and financial implications associated with these distinct stroke categories.</p></p>
